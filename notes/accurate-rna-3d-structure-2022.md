## TL;DR

RhoFold+ is an end-to-end deep learning method for RNA 3D structure prediction from sequence. It integrates RNA-FM (a BERT-style language model pre-trained on ~23.7M RNA sequences) with a Rhoformer module and an AlphaFold2-inspired structure module. It achieves sub-4 Å mean RMSD on RNA-Puzzles and outperforms all methods (including human experts) on CASP15 natural RNA targets, with inference in ~0.14 seconds per structure. Published in Nature Methods (2024).

## Model

- **Name**: RhoFold+
- **Architecture**: Three-stage pipeline: RNA-FM (12-layer BERT-style LM, 640 hidden, 20 heads) → Rhoformer (12-layer transformer processing MSA + LM embeddings, with gated self-attention and outer product attention) → Structure Module (8 IPA layers predicting local frames and torsion angles)
- **Input**: Single RNA sequence; optionally MSAs (max 256 sequences) built via Infernal + rMSA from Rfam/RNAcentral/nt databases
- **Output**: All-atom RNA 3D coordinates, secondary structure contact map, pLDDT confidence scores, inter-helical angles
- **Recycling**: 10 iterations of Rhoformer + Structure Module, halted by pLDDT convergence
- **Post-processing**: Optional AMBER/BRiQ force-field relaxation (does not improve RMSD/TM-score but removes stereochemical violations); secondary structure base-pairing constraints applied after full-atom reconstruction
- **Parameters**: Not explicitly reported. RNA-FM: 12 transformer blocks × 640 hidden × 20 heads (roughly ESM-scale for RNA)

## Data

- **Pre-training (RNA-FM)**: ~23.7M non-coding RNA sequences from RNAcentral (de-duplicated at 100% identity via CD-HIT-EST). 16 token vocabulary (A, C, G, U + ambiguity codes). Max sequence length 1,024 during training
- **Fine-tuning / 3D training**: 5,583 RNA chains from PDB (BGSU Representative Sets, 2022-04-13), clustered at 80% sequence identity → 782 clusters, 702 used for training. Filtered: resolution < 4.0 Å, length 16–256 nt, single-chain only
- **Self-distillation**: 27,732 sequences with ground-truth secondary structure from RNAStralign + bpRNA-1M (filtered to 16–256 nt). Pseudo 3D labels generated by initial RhoFold+ model; re-trained with 25% PDB + 75% distillation data. Residues with pLDDT < 0.7 masked during distillation training
- **Benchmarks**: RNA-Puzzles (24 single-chain targets), CASP15 (6 natural RNA targets), 10-fold cross-validation on full PDB set, 76 newly determined structures (post-training), cross-family/cross-type validation on Rfam families

## Training Recipe

- **RNA-FM pre-training**: Masked language modeling (15% masking, 80/10/10 mask/random/keep). 8× A100-80GB GPUs, 1 month. Inverse-square-root LR schedule, base LR 0.0001, weight decay 0.01, 10,000 warm-up steps
- **RhoFold+ training**: Adam optimizer, LR 0.0003, polynomial decay with 10,000 warm-up steps, batch size 16, dropout 0.1. 1,600 epochs / 300,000 iterations, ~1 week on 8× A100-80GB. GPU cluster total 768 GB memory
- **Loss function**: L = L_mlm + 0.3×L_dis + 0.1×L_ss + 0.03×L_clash + 2×L_FAPE + 0.1×L_ss3d + 0.01×L_pLDDT. Combines 1D (MLM on MSA), 2D (distance bins, secondary structure), and 3D (FAPE, base-pairing constraints, clash penalty, pLDDT evaluator) losses
- **Inference**: ~0.14 s per structure on single A100 (without MSA search time)

## Key Ablations & Design Choices (MOST IMPORTANT)

1. **MSA module is the most critical component.** Removing MSA caused the largest performance drop across all ablations. Performance inversely correlates with MSA depth; marked improvement when MSA count exceeds 100 sequences
2. **RNA-FM language model is the second most important component.** Removing RNA-FM led to a sharper performance decline for dissimilar sequences (slope 5.0 vs 3.4 for full model in RMSD vs 1/seq-sim regression). RNA-FM partially compensates for loss of MSA module (p=0.0005 for TM-score improvement when adding RNA-FM to MSA-free model)
3. **Self-distillation addresses data scarcity.** Using pseudo-3D labels from initial model + secondary structure ground truth, with pLDDT < 0.7 masking, improves performance. 25/75 PDB/distillation sampling ratio during re-training
4. **Recycling module matters most for long sequences.** Removing recycling (10 iterations) most significantly hurt predictions on longer RNAs, effectively acting as a model-deepening strategy
5. **Frame definition matters for RNA.** Direct prediction of base frames (C1′, N1/N9, C2/C4) caused convergence problems. Using backbone frame (C4′, C1′, N1/N9) + 4 torsion angles (α, β, γ, ω) resolved this
6. **MSA sampling enables multiple predictions.** Randomly sampling/clustering 256 MSAs from larger pools generates diverse models. Top-5 selection from MSA sampling yields up to 10 Å RMSD improvement over single default prediction
7. **Modified AlphaFold2 baseline (without MSA module) performs worse than RhoFold+**, confirming the combined value of RNA-FM + MSA integration
8. **No significant overfitting to training set.** R² values of 0.23 (TM-score) and 0.11 (lDDT) vs max sequence similarity on RNA-Puzzles indicate no strong correlation between training similarity and performance
9. **Secondary structure prediction emerges from Rhoformer features.** Attention maps from Rhoformer align with contact maps, enabling state-of-the-art secondary structure prediction via a post-processing module without dedicated secondary structure training

## Reported Insights

- RhoFold+ achieves 4.02 Å mean RMSD on RNA-Puzzles (24 targets), 2.30 Å better than second-best (FARFAR2: 6.32 Å). Average TM-score 0.574 vs 0.41–0.44 for competitors
- On CASP15 natural RNA targets, outperforms all methods including AIchemy_RNA2 (which required expert knowledge) by 0.06 Å average RMSD. Cumulative Z-score on par with AIchemy_RNA2
- On 76 newly determined structures, achieves 7.74 Å mean RMSD, ~0.8 Å better than DeepFoldRNA, ~2.2 Å better than AlphaFold3
- Cross-family validation: 6.69 Å average RMSD. Struggles with complex families (group I introns, CRISPR elements) that interact with proteins
- Cross-type validation: strong on tRNA (TM-score 0.73) and miRNA, weaker on introns and riboswitches
- pLDDT scores correlate well with prediction quality (Pearson -0.718 with C1' RMSD), useful as confidence metric
- Can correct crystallographic artifacts (e.g., domain-swapped dimers)
- Outperforms AlphaFold3 and RoseTTAFold2NA on single-chain RNA (these are designed for complexes)

## Notes / Open Questions

- Explicit parameter count for the full RhoFold+ model is not reported; would need code inspection to determine
- Limited to sequences ≤ 500 nt in practice; struggles with large/complex RNAs
- Cannot handle RNA complexes (protein-RNA, ligand-RNA); complementary to AlphaFold3/RoseTTAFold2NA
- Dynamic conformational ensembles (especially at junctions) not modeled
- Training data derived from specific environmental conditions (crystallization); may not generalize to in vivo solution conditions
- Published in Nature Methods 21, 2287–2298 (2024), doi:10.1038/s41592-024-02487-0
